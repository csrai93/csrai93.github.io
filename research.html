Research Philosophy

The idea of research for me is twofold - 1) It should be 'to introduce' rather than 'to produce'. 2) The outcome of research should be publication, not the other way round. I enjoy being involved in joint and open discussions with my fellow researchers and students on different technologies related to my field of interest, which ultimately leads to algorithm development.

Prospective areas of research

I am actively working on the radar systems currently. Specifically, I work on the target detection and parameter estimation with a smaller number of measurements. I am also working actively on drone detection and micro-motion parameter estimation. As a future interest, I am looking to work on the radar for healthcare systems.

During my PhD, I investigated the spatial wideband effects on the uniform linear antenna array based systems and applied Machine-Learning and Deep Learning tools mixed with classical array signal processing techniques for robust signature estimation of spatial wideband systems. As a continuation of this field, I am looking forward to working on near-field effects in communication and radar systems.
